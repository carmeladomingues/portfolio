Project Overview

An app that makes teamwork more rewarding.

This project was developed in the context of an academic PBL (Project-Based Learning). The challenge was to create a tool that would help teams manage team building dynamics in a structured and motivating way.

The app solves a real problem: the lack of visibility and recognition in team activities. With a points system, achievements, and redeemable odysseys, every member has a reason to actively engage.

The central question that guided everything: How might we bring creativity and innovation into organisations through collaboration?

Research

Listening before designing.

Before designing anything, we mapped how creativity and collaboration actually work — or fail — inside real organisations. Three tracks, one clear pattern.

Desk Research We analysed articles, reports, and case studies across five themes — Innovation, Design, HR, Collaboration, and Creativity. The literature was consistent: organisations that foster collaboration and psychological safety outperform those that don't. One insight surfaced repeatedly: innovation needs culture, not just tools.

Desk research clusters image

Interviews We conducted in-depth interviews with 10 professionals across different industries and work models — hybrid, remote, and in-person. Most felt their ideas weren't heard, cross-department collaboration was rare, and recognition was almost non-existent.

Ideas are rarely heard No cross-team collaboration
Recognition is almost non-existent Hybrid breaks spontaneous exchange
No collaborative tool for the whole org Motivation fades without connection

Survey 56 responses. Nearly 50/50 gender split, majority aged 18–25. 84% considered cross-department collaboration necessary — yet half said their teams only met weekly, and communication frequency was consistently rated as low. Work model (hybrid, remote, in-person) was not seen as the barrier — culture was.

Personas

Two people, one shared frustration.

Two personas were built to represent the range of people affected by this problem — different roles, different contexts, the same daily struggle with a disconnected workplace.

Miguel

29 years old · UX/UI Designer · Porto

"Working from home gives me freedom I never had, but I also miss the energy you feel in the office."

Context

Works hybrid at a tech company. Values work-life balance. Misses the spontaneous exchanges that happen in the office and help with idea sharing.

Pain

Feels a lack of knowledge sharing within his team. Struggles to stay connected with colleagues when working remotely.

Need

Regular team meetups and a way to stay connected with what colleagues are doing and thinking.

Pedro

37 years old · Interior Designer · Lisbon

"I love the freedom my company gives me with hybrid work — I can finally have a real work-life balance."

Context

Works hybrid at a small atelier for 7 years. Team of 3. When in the office, everyone works in the same room.

Pain

Doesn't know what other departments are working on. Collaboration is limited and mostly informal.

Need

More sharing among colleagues — projects, knowledge, and progress — because the lack of visibility limits creativity.

Problem

Too many ideas lost between floors.

The affinity diagram revealed a consistent pattern across all research tracks. People want to collaborate. They have ideas. But organisations lack the culture, tools, and moments to make it happen. Motivation fades. Communication breaks down. Creativity stays untapped.

WHO
Creative professionals in hybrid organisations who offer flexible work but lack the capacity to manage it collaboratively.
WHAT
Lack of sharing within hybrid work — projects, communication, and knowledge between departments and people.
WHERE
In creative agencies and companies with hybrid work models.
WHEN
When there are no regular cross-team meetings, no collaborative tool for the whole organisation, and communication is not valued as part of culture.
WHY
Because it affects team profitability, interferes with essential collaboration for growth, and reduces the stimulation of creativity and innovation.

Exploration

Every idea on the table.

We ran a broad ideation session — no filters, no limits. Solutions were then grouped into two clusters: tools and systems that improve collaboration, and activities that bring people together through shared experiences.

The concept that united both: a gamified platform that turns team dynamics into rewarding experiences — combining participation, recognition, and fun to foster the culture change organisations need.

Concept

Collaboration shouldn't feel like work.

The PBL App is a mobile platform built to make team dynamics visible, rewarding, and fun. Not a project management tool — a gamified experience designed around what actually motivates people: recognition, connection, and shared moments.

The concept is simple: give teams a reason to participate, celebrate every contribution, and turn collaboration into something people look forward to — not another obligation on the calendar.

⭐

Points System

Every completed dynamic earns points. Progress is visible and motivating, with a progression bar toward the next reward.

🗺️

Odysseys

Real rewards — dinner, cinema, spa — redeemable with accumulated points. Each odyssey has a clear cost and description.

🏆

Leaderboard

Visual team ranking with a top-3 podium. Each member can see their position and what it takes to climb.

📋

Project Briefing

Structured template for client projects — objectives, audience, phases. The team always knows where they stand.

📅

Next Dynamic

Full detail of the next activity — location, time, team, points to earn — with attendance confirmation and calendar add.

🎯

Achievements

Unlockable badges that recognise individual contributions: Top 3, Focus, Fast, Team. Visible on each member's profile.

Key design decisions

Consistent primary colour

The original prototype mixed cyan blue, orange, and white without clear hierarchy. The redesign unified everything around coral #FF6633 — warmer, more energetic, and aligned with the team building spirit.

Blue + Orange + White → Coral #FF6633

Header with context

The original header was just a name and avatar. The new header includes real-time stats (dynamics, points, ranking) giving users immediate context about their standing in the team.

Name + Avatar → Stats + Context

Profile filled with meaning

The original profile had a lot of empty space and only a points balance. The redesign added achievement badges, a progression bar, and a clear CTA — turning the profile into a source of motivation.

Isolated balance → Achievements + Progress

Odysseys in a grid

Odyssey cards were horizontal and cut off information. The new 2×3 grid shows more content without scrolling, with category filters and clear CTAs on each card.

Horizontal scroll → Grid with filters

Learnings

What this project taught us

This was one of the most complete academic projects of the year — combining real research, strategic thinking, and end-to-end product design. The process revealed how deeply culture shapes design decisions.

Beyond the app itself, the project confirmed something more important: solving collaboration problems is not a tool problem — it's a human and cultural one.

A few key lessons stayed:

Research shapes everything
The depth of the research defined every design decision — from the points system to the briefing template. Without the interviews and survey, we would have built the wrong thing.

Culture over tools
People don't disengage because they lack software. They disengage because they don't feel seen. The app needed to address recognition first, features second.

Consistency is not optional
A well-defined design system from the start saves time and makes the app recognisable. Colour, spacing, and typography must be decided before designing any screen.

New flows emerge from use
The Leaderboard and Next Dynamic screens weren't in the original prototype — they emerged from understanding how teams would actually use the app day to day.
